About

I am postdoc associate at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) with extensive expertise in air quality modeling, chemical mechanisms, and environmental data analysis. With a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering, my research focuses on improving atmospheric chemical mechanisms in air quality models, particularly related to wildfire smoke and air pollution exposure. I also developed advanced workflows and databases for integrating experimental data with model simulations, improving the accuracy of environmental models. Proficient in Python and SQL, I have applied these skills to large-scale datasets for research purposes.

  • Technical: Python, SQL, MATLAB, Fortran, Linux, Data Engineering
  • Model/Software: UCD/CIT, WRF, F0AM, SAPRC, MechGen, BenMAP
  • Language: Mandarin, English

Looking for an opportunity to work on air quality management and environmental policy, where I can apply my expertise in chemical mechanisms, data analysis, and environmental modeling to support regulatory compliance and improve public health outcomes.
